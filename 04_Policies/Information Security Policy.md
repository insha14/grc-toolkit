
# Information Security Policy

## Overview
The Information Security Policy establishes the organization’s commitment to protecting information assets and outlines the high-level principles that guide security practices. It defines expectations for safeguarding confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information and provides a foundation for all other supporting security policies and procedures. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who interact with organizational systems or data.

## Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that information security is aligned with organizational objectives, legal requirements, and industry best practices. It sets the framework for managing security risks, defining roles and responsibilities, and implementing appropriate controls to protect information assets against unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, and destruction.

## Scope
This policy applies to all information systems, applications, endpoints, networks, communication technologies, and data processed or stored by the organization. It covers both digital and physical forms of information, as well as activities conducted by internal staff and external service providers.

## Policy Requirements

### Governance and Responsibilities  
The organization shall establish roles and responsibilities for managing information security. A designated security function or team will oversee implementation of controls, risk assessments, incident management, and compliance activities. Employees are responsible for adhering to all security policies and reporting potential security issues.

### Asset Protection  
All information assets must be identified, classified, and protected according to their sensitivity and business value. Systems and data must have appropriate access controls, secure configurations, and safeguards against unauthorized use or disclosure.

### Access Control  
Access to information systems must be granted based on the principle of least privilege. User accounts must be uniquely assigned, periodically reviewed, and revoked promptly upon termination. Privileged accounts must have additional controls such as strong authentication and monitoring.

### Acceptable Use  
Employees must use organizational systems and information resources responsibly. Activities such as unauthorized software installation, misuse of data, bypassing security controls, or engaging in unlawful behavior are prohibited. Acceptable use guidelines should be communicated clearly to all users.

### Incident Management  
Security incidents must be reported immediately to the appropriate teams. The organization shall maintain an incident response plan to ensure timely detection, investigation, containment, and recovery from security incidents.

### Compliance  
The organization shall comply with applicable laws, regulations, contracts, and industry standards related to information security. Policies and controls must be reviewed periodically to ensure continued effectiveness and relevance.

## Enforcement  
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including suspension of system access, corrective actions, or termination, depending on severity. Third-party violations may result in contract review or termination.

## Review and Maintenance  
This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or whenever significant changes occur in the organization’s environment, technology, or regulatory landscape. Updates must be approved by executive leadership or the designated governance committee.
