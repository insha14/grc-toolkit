# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

## Overview
The Acceptable Use Policy establishes the rules for using organizational systems, networks, devices, applications, and data. Its purpose is to ensure that employees and third parties use technology resources responsibly and securely. This policy helps prevent misuse, reduce cybersecurity risks, and maintain the integrity and availability of information systems.

## Purpose
This policy provides clear guidance on appropriate and inappropriate use of organizational assets. It aims to protect the organization from legal liabilities, data breaches, security incidents, and operational disruptions. The policy also supports compliance with regulatory and contractual obligations.

## Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, interns, third-party vendors, and any individuals who access organizational resources. It covers all company-owned devices, personal devices used for work (BYOD), cloud platforms, software applications, and network connectivity.

## Policy Requirements

### Acceptable Use Guidelines
Users must use organizational resources only for authorized business purposes. Activities should support job responsibilities, comply with policies, and align with ethical and legal standards. Occasional personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work duties or violate any security requirements.

### Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited:
- Installing unauthorized software or applications  
- Bypassing security controls or attempting to gain unauthorized access  
- Sharing passwords or using another personâ€™s credentials  
- Downloading or distributing malicious, pirated, or inappropriate content  
- Using company resources for illegal, harmful, or disruptive activities  
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the network  

### Data Protection Responsibilities
Users must safeguard sensitive information by following secure handling practices, such as encrypting data where required, preventing unauthorized disclosure, and reporting suspicious activity. Screens must be locked when unattended, and physical documents should be stored securely.

### Device and Network Usage
Users must keep devices updated, follow endpoint security requirements, and avoid connecting to insecure networks without proper safeguards. Personal devices used for work must comply with security controls such as password protection and mobile device management (MDM) if applicable.

### Monitoring and Logging
The organization reserves the right to monitor system activity, network traffic, email communications, and device usage to ensure compliance with this policy. Monitoring activities shall be conducted in accordance with applicable laws and internal procedures.

## Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including warnings, access suspension, retraining, or termination. Severe violations may lead to legal consequences or reporting to authorities.

## Review and Maintenance
This policy shall be reviewed annually or when significant changes occur in technology, regulations, or organizational needs. All updates must be approved by the information security function or senior management.
